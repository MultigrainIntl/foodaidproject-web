Governance

Rebuilding with documented controls.

Food Aid Project is rebuilding its board. Historical bylaws and policies address conflicts of interest, compensation, ethics, gifts, whistleblowing and nondiscrimination.

A current board roster will be published after appointments are formally documented.

Related-party safeguards

Clear roles matter.

Food Aid Project, Multigrain International and GISit LLC are distinct organizations.

Potential related-party arrangements should be disclosed, evaluated under the conflict policy, considered against alternatives, approved by disinterested directors and recorded in minutes.

Use of support

No automatic “dollars become meals” promise.

Donations support the charitable mission, which may include nonprofit operations, program development, partnerships, evidence work and appropriately approved mission-related technology enhancements.

Specific restricted gifts will be described according to their documented purpose. Goals, projections and capacity estimates will not be presented as achieved outcomes.

Due diligence

Ask the difficult questions.

Funders, partners and donors are welcome to ask about status, finances, governance, programs and organizational relationships.
