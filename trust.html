Governance

Strengthening the institution.

Food Aid Project is rebuilding its board and formalizing the next stage of its work.

Historical bylaws and policies address conflicts of interest, compensation, ethics, gifts, whistleblowing and nondiscrimination. Current leadership and governance information will be updated as appointments and records are completed.

Organizational responsibility

Food Aid Project stands on its own.

Food Aid Project, Inc. is responsible for its charitable mission, programs, fundraising and partnerships.

Outside services, technology access and potential financial arrangements should be documented, reviewed and approved through appropriate nonprofit safeguards.

Use of support

Support the mission and the work required to build it.

Donations may support nonprofit operations, program development, partnerships, research and responsibly approved mission-related tools.

Restricted gifts will be described according to their documented purpose. Food Aid Project will not promise a specific result unless the gift and program are structured to support that promise.

Questions welcome

Ask about the organization.

Funders, partners and donors are welcome to ask about status, finances, governance, programs and current capacity.
