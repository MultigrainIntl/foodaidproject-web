Evidence & learning

Ambition earns trust when its evidence has a date, an owner and a limit.

We distinguish historical commercial work, nonprofit activity, independent collaboration, live tools and initiatives still in development.

2010 · Multigrain International

Three food-bank purchasing examples

The IRS narrative documented $19,500 in combined savings across transactions involving Bay Area Food Bank, Northern Illinois Food Bank and Three Square Food Bank. The narrative attributes this work to Multigrain International using an early decision tool and supplier network.

Use: historical proof of the problem and model—not current Food Aid Project impact.

2018–2019 · GISCorps

Edible-bean remote sensing

An independently documented volunteer project explored field identification and yield analysis. Small regional work produced useful results; insufficient data limited global expansion and the project concluded in December 2019.

Use: dated research with its limitation intact.

Current public availability

GISit Weather

The public weather gateway is reachable and names Food Aid Project as the supporting nonprofit. Ownership remains with GISit LLC and the broader program portfolio is in development.

Use: evidence of a live tool, not proof of beneficiary outcomes.

How claims are classified

Proven

Supported by an identified primary document, independently published source or directly observable public tool.

Historical

Accurate for a stated period but not presented as evidence of current activity.

In development

A real initiative being built, tested or organized. It is not described as fully operational or scaled.

Paused or canceled

Retained only when the history or learning is useful. Oatmeal 4 Hope is currently in this category.

Unknown

Not published as fact until the missing evidence or status is resolved.
