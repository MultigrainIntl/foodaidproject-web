What we do

We help make the decisions behind food security clearer.

Food insecurity is visible. The costly friction behind it often is not.

How food is grown, bought, moved and delivered depends on price, freight, crop conditions, timing, information and local capacity. Food Aid Project works across those connections.

That makes efficiency humanitarian: better procurement, lower waste, clearer information and earlier warning can help capable organizations do more with what they already have.

Working intelligence

Food security starts before food is scarce.

Weather and soil conditions influence what grows, what becomes available, what it costs and where pressure may emerge.

GISit makes changing conditions easier to see. The live view rotates through major agricultural regions using observed radar—one working capability within a broader decision-support direction.

Why Food Aid Project exists

Built from practical experience inside the food market.

Food Aid Project began with a simple observation: nonprofit food buyers did not always have the same information, relationships and tools available to experienced commercial buyers.

The organization was created to transfer useful knowledge, improve access and help capable organizations make stronger procurement and transportation decisions.
