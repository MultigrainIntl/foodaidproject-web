The overlooked problem

Hunger is visible. The friction behind it often is not.

Food-security organizations operate inside complex agricultural markets. Fragmented pricing, limited supplier access, freight constraints, weather, crop risk and scarce staff time all affect how far a food dollar can travel. Food Aid Project was created to work on those constraints.

Why Food Aid Project is different

Designed from inside the market—not outside the problem.

Food Aid Project grew from decades of work in agricultural commodities and the direct observation that food banks could pay more than necessary because they lacked the time, relationships and market intelligence available to experienced commercial buyers.

That perspective produced a different charitable model: transfer useful knowledge and technology into the food-aid sector, strengthen procurement and logistics, and help existing organizations extend their own reach.

Evidence before promotion

We separate history, current work and future potential.

Credibility requires more than large numbers. Our evidence approach attributes work to the entity that performed it, dates historical results, labels development honestly and identifies what remains to be validated.

2010 · Historical commercial evidence

Food-bank procurement

Multigrain documented $19,500 in combined savings across three food-bank purchasing examples. The work informed Food Aid Project’s model; it is not presented as current nonprofit impact.
