Technology & intelligence

Technology that helps people help people.

Food Aid Project uses technology to close gaps in information, expertise and resources—helping people understand difficult situations and make better choices more often.

The simple version

Better-supported people make stronger decisions.

Farmers, food organizations and communities with limited resources often face the greatest risks while having the least access to timely information and specialized expertise.

We design practical tools that organize complex information, explain risks and help people compare possible choices. A weather map matters when it helps someone recognize risk. A crop model matters when local knowledge can improve it. A delivered-cost tool matters when it helps an organization compare realistic options.

People remain at the center

Better-supported human decisions—not automated decision-making.

Artificial intelligence, mapping and data analysis can identify patterns and make difficult information easier to use. They cannot replace local knowledge, professional expertise, human judgment or responsibility.

The people closest to the problem remain the decision-makers. Technology supports the relationship between people; it does not replace it.
