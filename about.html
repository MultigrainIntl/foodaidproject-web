About Food Aid Project

Built from a view most food-security organizations never get.

Food Aid Project began with a commodities professional seeing the same problem repeatedly: organizations feeding people did not always have the time, supplier relationships, freight knowledge or decision-support tools available inside commercial agricultural markets.

Origin

Practical experience became a charitable idea.

George Jibilian founded Food Aid Project after decades in agricultural commodities merchandising and after observing food-bank purchases made above available market prices. In 2010, Multigrain International documented savings across food-bank transactions using supplier relationships, freight knowledge and an early price-and-freight decision tool.

The lesson was larger than any one shipment: valuable market intelligence existed, but access to it was uneven. Food Aid Project was formed to make practical procurement knowledge, technology and support available through a charitable model.

Not another food distributor

Food Aid Project works at the points where information changes outcomes: supply visibility, delivered cost, freight, crop conditions, market access and organizational capacity. It is designed to complement food banks, NGOs and local organizations—not replace the relationships and community knowledge they already possess.

Distinct entities, deliberately explained

Food Aid Project, Inc. is the 501(c)(3) public charity. It develops charitable programs, provides public-benefit access, raises charitable support and builds partnerships.

GISit LLC owns the technology George personally funds and develops. Food Aid Project and its programs receive free, limited access. Charitable funding may support future enhancements connected to the nonprofit mission, subject to appropriate governance and documentation.

Multigrain International, LLC is the separate for-profit commodities business. Its historical food-bank work helped demonstrate the need, but commercial transactions and nonprofit activities must remain clearly attributed and separately governed.

Governance in renewal

Food Aid Project is rebuilding its board. Current governance information will be published as appointments are formally documented.
