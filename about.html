About Food Aid Project

Connecting the decisions behind food and economic security.

Food security is shaped by more than food alone. Weather affects crops. Markets affect prices. Transportation affects access. Information affects every decision.

Our purpose

Help organizations see more clearly and use resources wisely.

Food Aid Project connects agriculture, markets, logistics, technology and people around practical food-system challenges.

The goal is to strengthen the people and organizations already doing the work. We use technology to make useful information and decision support more accessible—not to replace local knowledge, professional expertise or human connection.

Our mission

Food and economic security through better decisions.

See risk earlier. Compare options more clearly. Move resources wisely. Build knowledge and capacity that last.

The organization

Independent and mission-led.

Food Aid Project, Inc. is a 501(c)(3) public charity established in 2010. The nonprofit is responsible for its own mission, programs, fundraising and partnerships.

Food Aid Project is led by Director George Jibilian, whose background includes agricultural commodities, logistics and food-system technology.
